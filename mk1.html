Akash Mk-I

First Inducted Variant of the Akash Missile System

Overview

Akash Mk-I was developed under IGMDP to replace legacy Soviet-era air defense systems. It uses a command guidance system and is deployed by both the IAF and Army.

Specifications

  • Range: ~30 km
  • Altitude: 20 m to 18 km
  • Speed: Mach 2.5–3.0
  • Warhead: 60 kg fragmentation
  • Guidance: Command via Rajendra radar
  • Launch Platform: Tracked and wheeled vehicles

Operational History

After a decade of development and testing, Akash Mk-I was inducted into the IAF in 2010. The Indian Army followed with deployment in 2015. It protects strategic assets in border regions.
